Font requirement
If the characters in this page are not visible in Telugu, then it means you do not have even one suitable Unicode Telugu font in your system. To download and install atleast one FREE Unicode Telugu font, kindly visit this page or see azhagi.com's unicodeff.html for links to many such pages.

x, ` and X modifiers
  • x when applied after certain Telugu letters modifies them. For e.g.
    kruxpaa = కృపా, Rux = ౠ, 1x = ౧ (numeral 1)
  • Whenever x does not take the role of a modifier, it just prints 'క్ష్'. For e.g.
    laxman = లక్ష్మన్

  • ` when applied before certain Telugu consonants modifies them. For e.g.
    `g = ఙ్, `j = ఞ్
  • ` when applied between certain Telugu consonants (like k`h, g`h, t`h, d`h, etc.) helps them to form their natural conjuncts. For e.g.
    bakkiMghaam = బక్కింఘామ్ whereas bakkiMg`haam = బక్కింగ్హామ్

  • X can be applied between Telugu consonants to avoid forming the natural conjunct (which would have occurred otherwise). For e.g.
    vatsalaa = వత్సలా whereas vatXsalaa = వత్‌సలా
  • X can also be applied between certain Telugu vowels (say 'ai' or 'ou') to help them get separated into their component vowels. For e.g.
    priyaiMDiyaa = ప్రియైండియా whereas priyaXiMDiyaa = ప్రియ‌ఇండియా
